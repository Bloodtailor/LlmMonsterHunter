# What Is This?

## The pitch

It's a monster-catching adventure game — you explore, you find creatures,
you battle them, you convince some of them to join you, they grow and
change over time. That part will feel familiar if you've ever touched
Pokémon or a similar game.

Here's the twist: **none of the content exists until you play.**

There's no list of monsters hidden in the game files. No pre-written story.
No scripted battles. Instead, an **AI** (the same kind of technology behind
ChatGPT) invents everything in real time:

- The monsters — their looks, names, personalities, backstories, abilities
- The artwork for each monster's card (painted on the spot by an image AI)
- What happens when you fight — the AI narrates the battle and decides how
  it plays out
- The conversations — monsters actually talk to you, and mean it

So every creature you meet is genuinely one-of-a-kind. Nobody else who
plays will ever see the same monster you do.

## Why this is a strange and interesting idea

Most video games work like this: developers spend years hand-crafting
every monster, every line of dialogue, every possible outcome, and ship it
all inside the game. The game is basically a very fancy way of playing back
things humans already made.

This project bets on something different. The code here is deliberately
*empty* of content. It's more like a **stage manager** than a movie: it
keeps track of who's in your party, where you are, what's happened so far —
and then it hands all of that to the AI and says "okay, your turn, make
something happen." The AI is the writer, the artist, and the referee.

Aaron describes it as an experiment in a **new way of building software** —
where you spend the computer's effort on *thinking and imagining* instead
of on rendering fancy graphics or replaying pre-made assets.

## Two versions of the same experiment

There are actually two sibling projects exploring opposite bets:

- **This one (the "cloud" edition):** uses big, powerful AI models that run
  on the internet. Smarter, richer storytelling; costs a little money per
  session in AI usage.
- **A sibling project (the "local" edition):** tries to make the whole
  thing run on a small AI model on your own computer, for free — which
  means redesigning a lot to work within tighter limits.

You're reading about the cloud edition.

## The honest framing

This is a **personal project**, built by one person, for learning and as a
portfolio piece. It's not a commercial product and there's no company
behind it. It's the kind of thing you build because the idea won't leave
you alone.
