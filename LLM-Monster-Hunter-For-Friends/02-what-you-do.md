# What You Actually Do

Here's what a play session feels like, feature by feature.

## Start a new game and make your character

You create a player character — pick some options, or just describe yourself
in your own words, and even give yourself a portrait. You're a real
character *in* the world, part of your own party, and you can speak as
yourself in conversations.

## Go on expeditions (dungeon runs)

You head out on a run with a goal and some stakes. As you explore, you're
offered a few **mysterious paths** to choose between. Each one secretly
hides something:

- a place to explore
- a monster that poses you a riddle
- a battle
- treasure
- or a familiar face from a previous run

You don't know which until you commit. Runs have a **danger level**
(calm → risky → perilous) that shapes how rough things get.

## Meet monsters that are genuinely unique

Every creature is invented on the spot by the AI: its own species, look,
personality, history, and abilities — plus a **painted card** made by an
image AI. No two are the same, and the art actually reflects who they are.

## Fight battles that are told like a story

Combat is turn-based, but there's no wall of numbers. Instead of "HP: 47/80,"
a monster's condition is described in **words** — fresh, scuffed, wounded,
battered, critical. Same for energy: brimming, steady, strained, drained,
spent.

On your turn you can attack, defend, use an ability — **or type whatever
you want to do in plain English.** Try to blind it with sand, talk it down,
climb a tree. The AI referees whether your idea makes sense and narrates
what happens. Enemies get to be clever too — they can talk, plead, or flee
on their own.

## Talk monsters into joining you

Monsters don't get captured by force. They join **by their own will.** In
the middle of a battle you can bargain, threaten, or plead with a creature.
Whether it works depends on the monster — its personality, how the fight's
going, how it feels about you.

## Build real relationships

The monsters that join you **remember things** — battles you fought
together, conversations, journeys, defeats. Your bond with each one deepens
over time (wary → familiar → trusting → devoted). A monster that barely
tolerates you might act on its own in a fight; one that's devoted has your
back.

Even monsters you *defeated* can come back later — and they'll remember how
that went. They might return hostile, friendly, or somewhere in between.

## Hang out at the campfire

Back at home base you can just... talk to your monsters. Open-ended
conversations, no agenda. They remember what you discuss, and these chats
can go on as long as you like.

## Grow and evolve

Monsters earn small growth during runs. Back home, there's an **Evolution
Altar** — a bigger ceremony that transforms a monster into a new form, with
new artwork and an evolved personality, while staying *the same monster*
with all the same memories. (The new art is even painted using the old art
as a reference, so it still looks recognizably like itself.)

## Collect stuff

There are items and keepsakes — consumables you find in dungeons, gifts a
monster might hand you in conversation, trophies you earn from victories.
The AI decides what they do when you use them.

## Everything happens live

Because the AI is thinking in real time, you watch it happen: monster cards
reveal themselves as they're painted, battle narration streams in as it's
written. Nothing is pre-loaded — you're watching creation happen.
